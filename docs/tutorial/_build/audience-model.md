# Audience Model

Purpose:
Define who the tutorial is for, what each audience already understands, what they are missing, and what kind of proof convinces them.

## Primary audiences

### Managers

- Existing intuition:
  projects drift when ownership, planning, and release expectations stay implicit
- Missing formalization:
  named frameworks and reusable operating artifacts
- Likely objection:
  "This sounds like more process overhead."
- Best proof:
  show how hidden coordination cost becomes reusable operating memory

### Developers

- Existing intuition:
  ticket execution is often disconnected from product coherence and release quality
- Missing formalization:
  the system view of how docs, design, reviews, and validation fit together
- Likely objection:
  "This sounds like PM theater."
- Best proof:
  show that the mechanics reduce rework, archaeology, and unclear handoffs

### Non-Developers

- Existing intuition:
  projects get messy, fragile, and confusing very quickly
- Missing formalization:
  a model for why that happens and how to stabilize it
- Likely objection:
  "This sounds too technical or too heavyweight."
- Best proof:
  show that each framework exists because a very recognizable mess kept recurring

## Reading-path requirements

For each major lesson:

- the opener must be understandable without SDLC jargon
- the strategy must still satisfy a technical reader
- the tactic must point to a concrete repo artifact
- the proof must show behavior change, not just theory
